Research Report How I Built This Dashboard Adwait Reddy · April 2026
01 — Market overview

Market Pressure Story

Demand is supported by strong population growth while supply is improving but not fully relieving pressure — keeping prices elevated even as the rental market moves away from its most severe crisis settings.

Demand: Perth pop. +2.4% Supply: WA approvals trending up Market: listings still below last year Rental: vacancy stabilising, rents high
02 — Signal interpretation

Quick Read

SignalWhy it matters
Population growth outpacing other capitalsSupports both owner-occupier and rental demand, especially in accessible suburbs.
Median house price at $885kDetached housing remains expensive, pushing demand toward units, townhouses and infill.
Vacancy at 2.2%Better than the tightest phase, but still indicates competition in well-located markets.
Approvals rising but unevenFuture supply improving, though not all approvals turn into near-term completions.
Research angle: the best opportunities sit at the intersection of access, affordability, land potential and tenant depth — not simply "cheap" or "high-growth" suburbs.

08 — Advisory synthesis

What Should a Client Do?

Client questionResearch answer
Maximise land returnScreen for access first. Sites near train precincts or activity centres are strongest candidates for denser forms and product diversification.
Where will prices/rents go?Base case remains upward in 2026, but at different speeds by product and location. Units and well-located infill still have structural support.
Where should a FIFO worker live?Airport-corridor and inner-east suburbs rank strongly — they reduce transfer friction while preserving amenity and tenant depth.
What suits an immigrant household?Look for suburbs balancing rent level, family fit and access to transport/retail — rather than chasing CBD prestige.
Suburb-level metrics curated from public REIWA suburb pages. Airport access and suitability scores are analyst-built heuristics for portfolio storytelling, not official rankings. Planning feasibility depends on zoning, R-Codes, local scheme controls, servicing and site constraints.
09 — Scenario explorer

Market Scenario Explorer

A scenario-based decision support tool — not a live prediction engine. Uses predefined market conditions and structured analyst reasoning to show how an advisory team might test different housing futures before making a recommendation.

How to read this: each scenario combines a possible market shock or policy change with the dashboard's evidence base. The output shows likely direction, key drivers, assumptions and practical implications for landowners, renters, buyers and investors.
